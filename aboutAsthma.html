About asthma

Asthma is a chronic disease of the airways that makes breathing difficult. People with asthma have sensitive airways in their lungs which react to triggers, causing a flare-up.
Asthma affects people of all ages, but it most often starts during childhood. Asthma can't be cured, but you and your child can mitigate the effects of the symptoms by following an asthma action plan. This is a written plan you develop with your child's doctor to track symptoms and adjust treatment.
With proper treatment and precautions, even severe asthma can be kept under control.

Triggers

One way to help manage your child's asthma is to avoid asthma triggers. Kids can't avoid all triggers all the time, but to learn what those triggers are and then helping your child avoid them can keep asthma symptoms under control. Triggers are different for each child, but some of the common asthma triggers include:

  • Cover mattresses, pillows, and box springs with mite-proof covers.
  • Choose bedding made with synthetic materials.
  • Vacuum and dust your home often.

Dust Mites

Microscopic bugs,
lives in household dust

  • Keep pets out of your child's bedroom and playroom.
  • Encourage your child not to hug or kiss the animal.
  • Have someone other than your child wash or brush your pet.

Pets

Saliva, urine, or dander

  • Seal any cracks in walls and floors.
  • Wash recyclables before putting them in the bin.
  • Avoid saving boxes, paper bags, or newspapers in piles around your home

Cockroaches

Shed body parts, saliva and droppings

  • Clean any visible mold or mildew, anti-mildew paint.
  • Run a dehumidifier in the basement or other damp areas.
  • Avoid houseplants, which may harbor mold in their soil.

Mold

Sends spores into the air

  • Keep windows and doors shut during pollen season.
  • Weather forecast will give a daily pollen report.
  • Avoid letting your child mow the grass or rake leaves.

Pollen
