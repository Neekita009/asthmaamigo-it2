Asthma in Children

Asthma affects people of all ages, but it most often starts during childhood. Asthma can't be cured, but you and your child can mitigate the effects of the symptoms by following an asthma action plan. This is a written plan you develop with your child's doctor to track symptoms and adjust treatment.
With proper treatment and precautions, even severe asthma can be kept under control.

Statistics in Australia

  • 1 in 10 Australians have asthma – around 2.5 million.
  • It’s more common in males under the age of 15
  • Rate of Asthmatic children under the age of 15, hospitalised is at at 442 per 100,000 population

Victoria

The line graph shows the trajectory of Asthma in children of Victoria below the age of 12 from the year 2013 to 2018.
The bar graph shows years lost due to disability (YLD) or the burden of disease. YLD is a measurement of years of productive life lost due to disability. This graph shows the cumulative number of years which these children have spent in hospitals or taking rest due to the disease instead of going to school, playing or doing any other activity.

Weather Condition for Asthma in Victoria

Extreme weathers, sudden fluctuation in Weather and Humidity is one of the biggest triggers for Asthma Patients. Below, the bar graph and the Map shows the Average Number of Hours which were unhealthy and Hazardous based on the temperature, Humidity, and sudden weather changes for the asthmatic Patients in each month in 2018 for different regions in Victoria.
Asthma Amigo will help you identify such weather condition so that the required Precautions could be taken.
